✨ Data collection

Article reports for field measurement and dietary surveys were searched from “Web of Science”, “PubMed”, “ScienceDirect”, and “Taylor & Francis Online”. Statistic measures of the concentration of PFAS mixture or individual, including the means, mediums, standard deviations, minimum, maximum, number of samples, and time of sampling were scripted. The terrestrial food types were determined based on Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations (FAO) category, “Bovine Meat”, “Mutton & Goat Meat”, “Pigmeat”, “Poultry Meat”, “Meat, Other” (including edible offal, processed and cooked food), and “Eggs”. Those of aquatic food were categorized by “Meat, Aquatic Mammals”, “Aquatic Animals, Others”, “Mollusc”, “Crustacean”, “Freshwater fish” and “Marine fish”, of which “Marine fish” was further categorized into “Demersal”, “Pelagic”, and “Mix” (for both demersal and pelagic) to fit the FAO category. In addition, the processed fishery food would be categorized as “Cooked”, referring “Fish, Others” in FAO category. The categorization of fish was based on “FishBase” by searching its nick name or scientific name, if available in article, or based on the water types-lake, river, and ocean-of sampling sites. To ensure data quality, recovery and limit of measurement-limit of detection (LOD) and/or limit of quantification (LOQ) must be nominated in the article or its supplementary information. To confine the food type of gathering fitting our research scope-animal sourced food-we included only concentrations of aquatic animal muscle. The wild territorial animals were not included, unless they were hunted for food.

Major discovery

  • Data span 1987~2023
  • Top 7 frequently measured PFAS are PFOS, PFOA, PFNA, PFUnA, PFDeA, PFDoA, and PFHxS.
  • Aquatic food, especially freshwater fishes, usually contain more PFAS than terrestrial food.
  • PFAS concentration is generally reducing in aquatic food from 2000 to 2023.

Geographic pattern

  • PFAS in livestock meat is lower than that in aquatic food in all continents.
  • Aquatic food from North America, Europe, and East Asia has more PFAS than those from South America, Oceania, and Africa.
  • Fishes from inner land water contain more PFAS than that in fishes from pelagic ocean.

Time trend

  • PFAS in aquatic food was decreasing from 2000 to 2023, but not significantly changed in terrestrial food.
  • There might be 3 stages of PFAS reduction in aquatic food: before 2010 (not change), 2011-2015 (fast reduction), and after 2016 (not change).
  • PFOA in pelagic fishes from East Asia was increasing from 2015 to 2023.
